Cuisine

Egg Tarts Inspired by British custard tarts and Portuguese pastéis de nata, egg tarts arrived in Hong Kong in the 1940s. The flaky, buttery crust and smooth egg custard filling made them a beloved bakery snack.

Yum Cha Small dishes like dumplings and buns, traditionally served in teahouses with tea. It's a social dining experience also known as “dim sum,” popularized in Hong Kong for brunch gatherings.

Hong Kong milk tea Nicknamed "silk stocking tea" due to the cloth filter used in brewing. This strong black tea mixed with evaporated milk is a legacy of British colonial influence. It's a creamy, slightly bitter drink found in nearly every Hong Kong diner.

Char Siu A barbecued pork marinated in honey, soy sauce, and Chinese five-spice. With roots in southern China, it's a staple in Hong Kong roast meat shops, often served with rice or inside buns.

Pineapple buns Named for its golden, crackly sugar crust that resembles pineapple skin. Originating in post-war Hong Kong, it blends Chinese baking with Western influences and is often enjoyed warm with a thick slice of butter inside.
